# Pricing And Monetization

## Purpose

Use this skill to turn a product's value into a practical charging model. For indie products, prefer simple pricing that can be implemented and explained clearly.

## Workflow

1. Identify who pays, why they pay, and what outcome they value.
2. Choose the pricing model: free trial, freemium, subscription, usage-based, one-time, credits, or service-assisted.
3. Define the value metric: seats, projects, usage, outputs, storage, exports, automation, integrations, or support.
4. Draw the free/paid boundary.
5. Decide upgrade triggers inside the product.
6. Define cancellation, refund, failed payment, and entitlement states.
7. Produce pricing page and in-product upgrade requirements.

## Required Output

Return:

- Buyer and willingness-to-pay reason.
- Recommended pricing model.
- Free vs paid boundary.
- Suggested tiers or plans.
- Upgrade triggers.
- Entitlement rules.
- Required billing states.
- Trust and copy notes.
- Implementation checklist.

## Pricing Rules

- Charge near the moment users understand value.
- Use one primary value metric at first.
- Avoid more than 2-3 paid tiers in an early product.
- Do not hide cancellation or pricing details.
- Do not make the free plan so generous that serious users never upgrade.
- Do not make the free plan so limited that users cannot experience value.
