# Competitor Analysis

## Purpose

Use this skill to understand the market around a product idea. The goal is not to copy competitors; it is to identify the user's alternatives, expectations, willingness to pay, and a realistic differentiation wedge.

If current competitor information is needed, browse or use reliable sources when available.

## Workflow

1. Define the product category and target user.
2. Identify direct competitors, indirect competitors, and current workarounds.
3. Compare positioning, core promise, pricing, onboarding, features, proof, and target segment.
4. Extract pain points from reviews, support forums, testimonials, changelogs, and pricing complaints when available.
5. Identify gaps an indie product can realistically exploit.
6. Convert insights into product scope, landing page messaging, and pricing implications.

## Required Output

Return:

- Category and target user.
- Competitor list: direct, indirect, workaround.
- Comparison table.
- Common user expectations.
- Complaints and unmet needs.
- Differentiation opportunities.
- Features to match, avoid, or postpone.
- Positioning recommendation.
- Risks.

## Analysis Rules

- Compare against the user's real alternative, not only direct SaaS competitors.
- Include "doing nothing" or manual workflow when relevant.
- Do not recommend matching every competitor feature.
- Prefer a narrow wedge: faster setup, simpler workflow, specific niche, better output, lower price, privacy, local-first, integration, or founder-led service.
- Treat review quotes as evidence only when source quality is acceptable.

Read `references/competitor-template.md` for the table format and example.
