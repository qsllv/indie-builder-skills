# Landing Page Section Patterns

Use these patterns to design or rewrite landing page sections. Adapt to the product, audience, and traffic source.

## Hero

Job: orient the visitor and make the core value obvious.

Must include:

- Target user or use case.
- Product category or recognizable task.
- Concrete outcome.
- Primary CTA.
- Product, result, or workflow visual.

Useful headline shapes:

- "The [category] for [audience] who need [outcome]."
- "Turn [messy input/problem] into [valuable output/result]."
- "[Achieve outcome] without [painful workaround]."
- "Know [important thing] before [costly moment]."

Weak hero signs:

- Headline is only a slogan.
- Subheadline repeats the headline.
- Visual does not show the product or result.
- CTA text does not reveal the next step.

## Problem

Job: make the user feel understood.

Show:

- The current workaround.
- The cost of that workaround.
- The repeated moment where pain appears.
- Why existing alternatives are insufficient.

Pattern:

"Right now, [user] has to [painful action] whenever [trigger]. That means [cost], [risk], and [friction]."

## Solution

Job: explain how the product changes the user's workflow.

Pattern:

"Instead of [old workflow], [product] lets you [new workflow], so you can [outcome]."

Keep this section concrete. Avoid explaining every feature.

## Benefits

Job: translate capabilities into outcomes.

Use 3-5 benefits. Each benefit should contain:

- A plain-language heading.
- A specific scenario or result.
- Optional proof or example.

Good benefit headings:

- "Find the real churn reasons."
- "Send reports without rebuilding charts."
- "Prioritize requests by revenue impact."

Weak benefit headings:

- "Powerful analytics."
- "AI automation."
- "Better collaboration."

## Feature Blocks

Job: explain what the product can do after value is already clear.

Structure each feature as:

- Feature name.
- What the user does with it.
- What improves because of it.

Example:

"Feedback clustering: Group hundreds of support messages by repeated themes, so product teams can see which problems affect the most customers."

## How It Works

Job: reduce perceived complexity.

Use 3-4 steps:

1. Connect or upload the input.
2. Configure or choose the workflow.
3. Review generated output or insights.
4. Share, export, publish, or act.

Avoid too many implementation details unless the audience is technical and evaluating feasibility.

## Proof

Job: make the promise believable.

Proof options:

- Real product screenshots.
- Example outputs.
- Customer quotes with specific details.
- Before/after comparisons.
- Usage metrics.
- Case studies.
- Integration logos.
- Security or compliance claims.
- Public demos.

Match proof to the claim. A claim about saving time needs time evidence or workflow comparison. A claim about quality needs example outputs. A claim about trust needs customer or security evidence.

## Use Cases

Job: help different visitors recognize themselves.

Use when the product serves multiple scenarios but still has one core value.

Structure:

- Role or situation.
- Trigger moment.
- Product action.
- Result.

Avoid turning use cases into a generic grid of personas with no concrete workflow.

## Comparison

Job: clarify why this product is better than alternatives.

Compare against:

- Current manual process.
- Spreadsheet or generic tools.
- Internal build.
- Competitors.
- Agencies or consultants.

Use concrete decision criteria:

- Setup time.
- Accuracy.
- Maintenance effort.
- Cost.
- Collaboration.
- Control.
- Speed to result.

Do not use dishonest competitor takedowns.

## Objection Handling

Job: remove friction before the CTA.

Common FAQ prompts:

- "How long does setup take?"
- "Do I need technical skills?"
- "What data do you store?"
- "Can I cancel anytime?"
- "What happens if the output is wrong?"
- "Does it work with my tools?"
- "How is this different from [alternative]?"

Answer with concrete policy, workflow, or limitation. Avoid vague reassurance.

## Final CTA

Job: summarize the value and ask for action.

Include:

- One-sentence promise.
- Primary CTA.
- Risk reducer if relevant.

Examples:

- "Create your first report in under 10 minutes."
- "Analyze a sample of your feedback before connecting live data."
- "Start a 7-day trial. No credit card required."
